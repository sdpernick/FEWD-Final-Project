Kinship

Neighborhood: Bloomingdale/Shaw (Convention Center)

Metro Station: Mt. Vernon Square

Cuisine: American

Price: $$$ ($40+)

Good For: Parents

Atmosphere: Fancy

When a friend suggested we check out Kinship, the new Eric Ziebold venture, in the rapidly growing Convention Center area, I looked at menu with great anticipation. This is a chef who worked for ten years at the three Michelin star restaurant, French Laundry. The man presumably knows his way around cuisine. Upon review of the menu, I couldn't help but to roll my eyes. The menu is divided into categories such as 'craft', 'history' and 'indulgence'.

Don't judge a book by its' pretentious cover. The meal was nothing short of phenomenal, the service attentive yet not obsequious, the atmosphere charming.

My dining companion and I embraced the 'medium plates' style as if they were 'small plates' and ordered four dishes: Maine Lobster toast, sweetbreads, sunchokes and truffle pasta.

Although the menu is forever evolving, the lobster toast is a staple and must-try (except for our vegan/vegetarian friends). Its' melt-in-your-mouth creaminess combined with the salty seafood taste is nirvana in each bite. I found myself wishing we had ordered two. I'm not really a sweetbread person, but their preparation made them more palatable. My friend, an avid consumer of sweetbreads, couldn't stop raving. The sunchokes were covered in a vivid maroon sauce and were prepared the way vegetables should be prepared-unrecognizable as healthy. And finally, the truffle pasta. A waiter placed dish of pasta and white sauce on our table and another waiter materialized out of nowhere with a grater. In his hand, however, was not a block of parmesan, but rather a large black truffle. He shaved and shaved, a most generous portion.

Completely satisfied, we were ready to call it a night. But then, the chocolate chip souffle was calling our name. Priced at a hefty $22, we weren't sure about our choice, until it arrived at the table. Imagine a soft, warm bed of chocolate chip cake with a crunchy crust and light, sweet whipped cream. That's exactly what we experienced as we wolfed it down.

Overall, wonderful spot not to be missed. But, it is quite pricey. It's also a small restaurant so best for groups of no more than six, ideal for 2-4. Grab friends who are enthusiastic about fancy ingredients, family who is picking up the check or a special occasion with a significant other.
